# Layout Audit

Use this checklist when reviewing an existing SCI figure, PPT slide, PDF page, or screenshot.

## 1. Scientific Story

- State the figure conclusion in one sentence.
- Mark each panel as: claim-setting, hero evidence, mechanistic support, validation, control, or context.
- Flag panels that do not serve the conclusion or duplicate another panel.
- Check whether the reading order matches the evidence order.

## 2. Hierarchy

Good large figures usually have visible evidence hierarchy:

- one dominant panel or panel group;
- a clear left-to-right or top-to-bottom path;
- secondary panels grouped by experiment type or question;
- detail panels smaller but still legible.

Red flags:

- all panels are equal size despite unequal importance;
- the eye jumps randomly between plots;
- a low-value schematic dominates the space;
- color bars, legends, or axis labels compete with data;
- panel letters are easier to see than the result.

## 3. Grid and Spacing

Audit these:

- outer margins are smaller than inter-panel gaps;
- gutters are consistent inside each panel group;
- group spacing is larger than within-group spacing;
- panel edges align on shared axes where possible;
- plots with shared comparisons have matched dimensions;
- microscopy or image rows have consistent crop aspect ratios.

Use fewer grid columns when text or axes become cramped. A figure that needs eight tiny panels may need two figures or a supplementary figure.

## 4. Typography

Target a restrained hierarchy:

- panel letters: bold, consistent, placed at the same relative anchor;
- in-panel labels: short, sentence case when possible;
- axis labels: readable at final print width;
- tick labels: smaller than axis labels but not hairline;
- statistical labels: consistent symbols and bracket style.

Avoid large per-panel titles. Move explanation into the figure legend unless a short label directly helps reading.

## 5. Visual Consistency

Check consistency across:

- group colors and control/treatment mapping;
- font family;
- axis line weight;
- marker size;
- error bar style;
- color bar size and tick formatting;
- scale bar length, color, and placement;
- annotation arrow style.

Do not recolor scientific categories casually. If colors encode biology, preserve the mapping.

## 6. Image Integrity

For microscopy, gels, blots, and histology:

- preserve scale bars and magnification labels;
- keep comparable contrast adjustments across compared images;
- avoid crops that remove relevant context;
- align groups in rows or columns with labels outside the image when possible;
- keep image borders subtle or absent unless needed to separate bright backgrounds.

## 7. Export Risk

Inspect final output at:

- full page view, to judge story and hierarchy;
- final column width, to judge readability;
- 200 percent zoom, to catch raster blur, jagged text, and alignment errors.

Flag low-resolution raster panels, embedded screenshots of plots, non-editable text, missing fonts, and inconsistent color profiles.
