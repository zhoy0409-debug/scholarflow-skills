# Scenario: Competition

Research competitions as rule- and rubric-driven writing tasks. This scenario
covers mathematical modeling, data science, innovation, and research-style
competitions.

## Official Sources

Find and record:

- competition website,
- rules,
- problem statement,
- scoring rubric,
- submission template,
- page limits,
- eligibility and formatting constraints,
- official notices,
- winning or high-scoring examples when available.

## Example Learning

Learn:

- how high-scoring entries state assumptions,
- how models or methods are justified,
- how results are visualized,
- how sensitivity, robustness, or limitations are presented,
- how the executive summary or abstract is written.

Competition writing should optimize for the stated evaluation criteria, not for
generic journal style.
